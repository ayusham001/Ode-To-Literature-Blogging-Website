A Station



This station of surrealism and sadness
Presented to me a please,
A juxtaposition of such hues
That belonged to...not here;
Those certain colors, that certain blue.
Was it the rain?
The reason for such beauty,
All the same of my rising rue.
The rain of cynical
Falling on the roof
And the warming red on the walls
As if offered themselves to be imbued
With the colors nocturnally brewed.
Or were it the flashing C-043s?
That posed me a neon paranoia of the new.
The fluorescence of flaring lunacy,
Reflecting on that cement slightly skewed.
Those swampy steel benches with apparitions
Of the people walking through the avenue
On their sulky steps of half sleep and half madness.
Surrealism filled the station
And so did the strange sadness
Which I had in me yet never knew.
