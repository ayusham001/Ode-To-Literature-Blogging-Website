A call to VIRTUAL YOU!




You asked me for a forever,
Which I can't ever promise.
But in time I wanted to be yours,
Yet you, to be mine.
So, I wrote a poem about us,
A poem about the brown of your eyes;
Which when the sun falls on
Shines like that of a wolf's,
Who's been in love since Christ.
About the rays of its gaze,
Which renders me a beauty
As if it's itself Aphrodite.

I composed too, out of your curled lashes
A rhythm of two intermitted waves;
The ones that makes my heart race,
Through the love and
My sweetest envious wonder
That I took to them everytime, whenever.

In the verses played also a child;
The child whom, in yourself you possess,
So naturally and so effortless.
The innocence of whom
Was shaped by Astraea herself,
The innocence that I always want to protect.

But then the poem turned long enough
That I halted and recited it to you,
I told and retold
For the world to hear it too.
And when it ended I promised my forever,
I burned the pages of you and me
Turning us into embers
That will fly ever together.
