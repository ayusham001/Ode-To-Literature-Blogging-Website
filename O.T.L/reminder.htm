Ode to Literature


A Promise




You're worried about your nose?
And worried about how you
Never in your life got a rose?

But what about those white flowers?
That I once gave you
For your eyes reminded me of the stars
Which they still, and will always do.

The white flowers were the closest
Most beautiful thing that I could find
Next to your eyes; the star dust,
Of wintery white glimmer kind.

You're worried about your teeth?
And worried about how you
Never in your life have seen a heath?

But what about those piddly places?
That we travelled together, hand in hand
For your palms felt like the good graces
Of the warm summer sand.

The excuses I made to hold them,
And keep holding onto, like an infant
Holding its mother's clothing hem,
And keeping forever its sweetly scent.

You're worried about what?
And you're worried why?
Don't you see my thoughts
And how your insecurities are a lie?
